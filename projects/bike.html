Multi-Load Structural Analysis of Bicycle Frame

Static structural analysis was performed using ANSYS Mechanical to evaluate deformation and stress distribution across multiple materials under realistic loading scenarios.

Material Comparison – Governing Load Case (Rear Wheel Bearing)

Structural Steel

Aluminum 6060-T4

Titanium Alloy

Carbon Fiber

Engineering Interpretation

The analysis compared total deformation and equivalent (von Mises) stress distribution across four structural materials.

  • Steel demonstrated high rigidity with low deformation.
  • Aluminum exhibited increased deformation due to lower modulus of elasticity.
  • Titanium provided balanced stiffness with favorable strength-to-weight ratio.
  • Carbon fiber showed lightweight advantage but localized stress sensitivity.

Other Load Cases Evaluated

In addition to rear wheel loading, the following scenarios were analyzed:

  • Vertical Loading
  • Startup Acceleration
  • Rear Braking
  • Horizontal Loading

Across all load cases, stress concentrations were primarily observed at joint intersections and connection points, indicating geometric discontinuity effects.
